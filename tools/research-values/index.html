House rules

For the nitpickers who refuse to answer an under-specified question — the looseness is intentional. A few conventions so we're playing the same game:

  • Assume your own situation as the baseline — your current pay, timezone, field, career stage — unless a card says otherwise.
  • Pick the reading that makes the choice hardest for you. If a card is only easy because you imagined the convenient version, you're dodging it.
  • The ambiguity is the instrument. Which missing detail you reach for, and why, is the actual signal. Name your assumption out loud (or in the note box).
  • Stuck for real? Use “genuinely can't choose” — a 50/50 split is recorded as a live tension, which is often the most interesting result.
  • A few genuinely number-dependent cards carry a “pin this down” note with the exact assumption baked in.
